Bio (Google Scholar Citations) (Github site)

Dr. Jian Wang received his B.S. and M.S. degrees from Harbin Institute of Technology, China, and the Ph.D. degree from Korea University, Korea, in 2006, 2009, and 2013, respectively. After graduation, he held positions as postdoc and assistant research professor at Rutgers University, Duke University, USA, and Seoul National University, Korea. Since 2017, he has been with the School of Data Science, Fudan University, China, where he is currently an associate professor. His research interests include

  • statictical and deep learning theory

  • graph signal processing and graph convolutional networks (http://fdu-iis-gnn.info)

  • sparse and low-rank recovery and its applications in optical imaging

  • multi-modal fusion perception for self-driving automobile

  • The relevant mathematics includes high-dimensional statistical theory, applied linear algebra, convex optimization, signal processing and information theory. He was nominated to the 2016 Young Author Best Paper Award in the IEEE Signal Processing Society.
